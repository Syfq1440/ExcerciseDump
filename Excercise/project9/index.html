Muhammad Ali

The greatest of all time!

Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali career&life:

  • 1942 - Born on January 17 in Louisville, Kentucky
  • 1954 - Clay made his amateur boxing against local amateur boxer Ronnie O'Keefe. He won and went on to win six Kentucky Golden Gloves titles, two national Golden Gloves titles, an Amateur Athletic Union national title, and the light heavyweight gold medal in the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome.
  • 1960 - Clay made his professional debut on October 29, winning a six-round decision over Tunney Hunsaker. From then until the end of 1963, Clay amassed a record of 19–0 with 15 wins by knockout.
  • 1963 - Clay join the heavyweight boxing champ
  • 1964 - Clay won his first match against Liston the current heavyweight title at that time and claiming the heavyweight title at age 22 upon winning he shouted: "Eat your words!" He added, "I am the greatest! I shook up the world. I'm the prettiest thing that ever lived."
  • 1965 - Soon after the Liston fight, Clay changed his name to Cassius X, and then later to Muhammad Ali upon converting to Islam and affiliating with the Nation of Islam. Ali then faced a rematch with Liston scheduled for May 1965 in Lewiston, Maine.
  • 1965 - Ali defended his title against former heavyweight champion Floyd Patterson on November 22, 1965. Although Ali clearly had the better of Patterson, who appeared injured during the fight, the match lasted 12 rounds before being called on a technical knockout.
  • 1966 - After the Patterson fight, Ali founded his own promotion company, Ali and then-WBA heavyweight champion boxer Ernie Terrell had agreed to meet for a bout but the Illinois Athletic Commission refused to sanction the fight. Instead, Ali traveled to Canada and Europe and won championship bouts.
  • 1967 - Ali fought Terrell in Houston on February 6. Terrell, who was unbeaten in five years and had defeated many of the boxers Ali had faced, was billed as Ali's toughest opponent since Liston
  • 1967 - On April 28, Ali appeared in Houston for his scheduled induction into the U.S. Armed Forces, but he refused, Ali refused to budge when his name was called, and he was arrested. Later that same day, the NYSAC suspended his boxing license and stripped him of his title, later June 20 the jury found Ali guilty after only 21 minutes of deliberation of the criminal offense of violating the Selective Service laws by refusing to be drafted.
  • 1971 - Ali and Frazier's first fight, held at the Garden on March 8, 1971, was nicknamed the "Fight of the Century" and on same year basketball star Wilt Chamberlain challenged Ali to a fight, and a bout was scheduled for July 26.
  • 1974 - World heavyweight champion "The Rumble in the Jungle" Ali fought against George Foreman, Chuck Wepner, Ron Lyle, and Joe Bugner.
  • 1976 - Ali fought Ken Norton. The bout,resulted in Ali winning a heavily contested decision that was loudly booed by the audience. Afterwards, he announced he was retiring from boxing to practice his faith
  • 1977 - After returning to beat Alfredo Evangelista in May 1977, Ali struggled in his next fight against Earnie Shavers that September, getting pummeled a few times by punches to the head. Ali won the fight, but the bout caused his longtime doctor Ferdie Pacheco to quit after he was rebuffed for telling Ali he should retire.
  • 1978 - In February 1978, Ali faced Leon Spinks at the Hilton Hotel in Las Vegas. A rematch occurred in September at the Superdome in New Orleans, Louisiana. 70,000 people attended the bout and paid a total of $6 million admission, making it the largest live gate in boxing history at that time.[120] Ali won a unanimous decision in an uninspiring fight, with referee Lucien Joubert scoring rounds 10-4, judge Ernie Cojoe 10-4, and judge Herman Preis 11-4. This made Ali the first heavyweight champion to win the belt three times.
  • 1979 - Ali announced his retirement from boxing again but Ali announced his comeback to face Larry Holmes for the WBC belt in an attempt to win the heavyweight championship an unprecedented fourth time.
  • 1980 - Holmes easily dominating Ali, who was weakened from thyroid medication he had taken to lose weight. The Holmes fight is said to have contributed to Ali's Parkinson's syndrome. By the end of his boxing career Ali had absorbed an estimated 200,000 hits
  • 1984 - After force retire Ali focus more on to better himself, Ali studies more about the Quran, to strengthen his beliefs with the help of Malcolm X, who soon became his spiritual and political mentor while becoming a Philanthropy, humanitarianism and politics
  • 2001 - Ali health started to declining
  • 2016 - dies at the age of 74.
