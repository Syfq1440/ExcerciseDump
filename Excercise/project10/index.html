Al-Nasir Salah al-Din Yusuf ibn Ayyub better known simply as Salah ad-Din or Saladin

"Righteousness of the Faith"

  • 1137 - born in Tikrit, Iraq
  • 1139 - his family left Tikrit. Ayyub and his family moved to Mosul
  • 1140 - Saladin, who now lived in Damascus, was reported to have a particular fondness for the city, but information on his early childhood is scarce. According to his biographers, Anne-Marie Eddé and al-Wahrani, Saladin was able to answer questions on Euclid, the Almagest, arithmetic, and law, but this was an academic ideal. It was his knowledge of the Qur'an and the "sciences of religion" that linked him to his contemporaries, several sources claim that during his studies he was more interested in religious studies than joining the military
  • 116* - Saladin's military career began under the tutelage of his uncle Asad al-Din Shirkuh
  • 1164 - Saladin, at age 26 join the Battle of al-Babein, the Crusader-Egyptian force and Shirkuh's army were to engage on the desert border of the Nile, just west of Giza. Saladin played a major role, commanding the right-wing of the Zengid army
  • 1169 - al-Adid appointed Saladin to replace Shawar as vizier. There was few minor dispute but in end saladin prevail and towards the end of the year, Saladin, with reinforcements from Nur ad-Din, defeated a massive Crusader-Byzantine force near Damietta.
  • 1170 - After establishing himself in Egypt, Saladin launched a campaign against the Crusaders, besieging Darum
  • 1173 - Saladin with reinforcements under Turan-Shah, Saladin's brother. Assisting Aswan his friend and teachers Emir of Egypt to fought in siege against the Nubian army, They manage to fend off the siege 2 times and the nubian were again driven off.
  • 1174 - "Conquest of Damascus" Saladin collected his own troops outside Cairo. the syrians request the aid of Saladin, who complied, On 23 November, he arrived in Damascus amid general acclamation and rested at his father's old home there, until the gates of the Citadel of Damascus, whose commander Raihan initially refused to surrender, were opened to Saladin four days later, after a brief siege by his brother Tughtakin ibn Ayyub. He installed himself in the castle and received the homage and salutations of the inhabitants.
  • 1175 - Saladin further conquests in Syria, Saladin's control of the provinces of Damascus, Homs, and Hama, as well as a number of towns outside Aleppo such as Ma'arat al-Numan. After his victory against the Zengids, Saladin proclaimed himself king and suppressed the name of as-Salih in Friday prayers and Islamic coinage.
  • 1177 - After fortifying and reconstructing Cairo. he set out upon a raid into Palestine; the Crusaders had recently forayed into the territory of Damascus, so Saladin saw the truce as no longer worth preserving.
  • 1178 - Saladin fought with the crusade army, his forces in Hama won a victory over their enemy and brought the spoils, together with many prisoners of war, to Saladin who ordered the captives to be beheaded for "plundering and laying waste the lands of the Faithful". He spent the rest of the year in Syria without a confrontation with his enemies
  • 1179 - Saladin once again fought the crusade army under King Baldwin on Jacob fortress, The engagement ended in a decisive Ayyubid victory, and many high-ranking knights were captured. Saladin then moved to besiege the fortress, which fell on 30 August 1179
  • 1182 - "Wars against Crusaders" Saladin crossed the Jordan River and moved westwards. Saladin sent out 500 skirmishers to harass the main Crusader force under Guy of Lusignan.
  • 1185 - Raynald of Châtillon, in particular, harassed Muslim trading and pilgrimage routes but is retaliate by Saladin.
  • 1187 - Saladin captured most of the Kingdom of Jerusalem and had captured almost every Crusader city. Saladin preferred to take Jerusalem without bloodshed and offered generous terms
  • 1193 - Saladin died of a fever at Damascus
